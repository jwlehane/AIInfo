1. Purpose

This policy outlines the guidelines for the responsible, ethical, and productive use of Artificial Intelligence (AI) tools at [Company Name]. Our goal is to leverage AI to enhance creativity, improve efficiency, and support our work in [describe core business activities, e.g., delivering excellent products/services], while safeguarding our company, employee, and client interests.

2. Scope

This policy applies to all [Company Name] employees, contractors, and anyone acting on behalf of [Company Name] when using AI tools for company-related work.

3. Guiding Principles

  • Innovation and Efficiency: We encourage exploring AI tools to improve our processes, generate creative ideas, and streamline repetitive tasks.
  • Human Oversight is Key: AI is a tool to assist, not replace, human judgment and expertise. All AI-generated content, designs, and decisions with significant impact must be reviewed and approved by a human.
  • Protecting Confidentiality: We must protect [Company Name]'s proprietary information and our clients' sensitive data when using AI tools.
  • Respecting Intellectual Property: We will respect copyright and intellectual property rights when using AI and in the work we produce.
  • Ethical Use: We are committed to using AI in a way that is fair, unbiased, and reflects our company values.
  • Transparency: We will be transparent about our use of AI where appropriate and required.

4. Approved AI Tools & Usage

General Use:

Employees may use AI tools for tasks such as:

  • Generating initial concepts or brainstorming ideas.
  • Assisting with drafting content (e.g., emails, reports, marketing copy) â€“ always requiring human review and editing.
  • Researching information and trends.
  • Automating simple or repetitive tasks where appropriate and with oversight.
  • Analyzing data (with appropriate safeguards for sensitive information).

Specific Tools:

A list of currently approved AI tools will be maintained by [Specify Person/Department, e.g., IT Department, Management Team]. Employees wishing to use a new AI tool for work purposes must request and receive approval from [Specify Person/Department] before use to ensure it meets our standards for security, privacy, and IP.

Prohibited Uses:

  • Do not input highly sensitive or confidential client information (e.g., financial data, personal identification information, trade secrets) into public or unsecured AI tools. Only use AI tools that have been vetted and approved for handling such information.
  • Do not use AI to generate content that is discriminatory, offensive, illegal, defamatory, or that could harm the reputation of [Company Name] or its clients.
  • Do not present AI-generated work as solely your own creation without internal acknowledgment of AI assistance. Transparency with clients regarding significant AI use in deliverables will be determined on a case-by-case basis by management.
  • Do not use AI for any purpose that could compromise the security of [Company Name]'s network, company data, or client data.
  • Do not use AI in a way that violates any applicable laws or regulations.

5. Data Privacy and Confidentiality

  • Exercise extreme caution with all data input into AI tools. Avoid using personally identifiable information (PII), protected health information (PHI), or other sensitive data unless the AI tool is specifically approved for such purposes and has robust, verified security and privacy measures in place.
  • For projects involving any personal or sensitive data, ensure all information is handled in strict accordance with privacy best practices, client agreements, and relevant regulations (e.g., GDPR, CCPA, HIPAA if applicable).
  • Default to anonymizing data or using generic placeholders when experimenting with AI for general concept development.

6. Intellectual Property (IP)

  • While AI can assist in the creation process, the final output, especially when significantly modified and refined by human expertise, may be considered the intellectual property of [Company Name] or its clients, as defined by contractual agreements.
  • Ensure that AI-generated elements (e.g., images, text snippets, code) do not infringe on existing copyrights, trademarks, or patents. Prioritize using AI tools that provide clear information on the commercial usage rights of their outputs or that generate verifiably original work.
  • Be aware that the legal landscape regarding IP ownership of AI-generated content is evolving. Consult with [Specify Person/Department, e.g., Legal Counsel if available, or Senior Management] if you have questions about IP rights.

7. Accuracy, Quality, and Human Oversight

  • AI-generated content can sometimes be inaccurate, incomplete, biased, or contextually inappropriate ("hallucinations").
  • All AI-generated outputs that will be used for decision-making, client deliverables, or public dissemination must be critically reviewed, fact-checked, and meticulously edited by an experienced employee before use.
  • The employee utilizing the AI tool is ultimately responsible for the accuracy, quality, and appropriateness of the final output. Our commitment to delivering high-quality, error-free work remains paramount.

8. Security

  • Use strong, unique passwords for any AI tools that require an account. Enable multi-factor authentication (MFA) wherever available.
  • Be cautious of phishing scams or malicious AI tools. Only download or access AI tools from reputable sources and, where applicable, ensure they are on the approved list.
  • Report any suspected security vulnerabilities, data breaches, or unusual AI tool behavior related to company or client data to [Specify Person/Department, e.g., IT Support, Security Officer] immediately.

9. Training and Awareness

[Company Name] may provide guidance, resources, or training on the responsible and effective use of approved AI tools. Employees are encouraged to stay informed about AI capabilities, limitations, and best practices relevant to their roles.

10. Policy Review and Updates

This AI policy will be reviewed periodically and may be updated as AI technologies and best practices evolve. Employees will be notified of any significant changes.

11. Policy Violations

Failure to comply with this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or contract, and may also carry legal consequences depending on the nature of the violation.

12. Questions

If you have any questions about this policy, how it applies to a specific situation, or whether a particular AI tool is appropriate for use, please contact [Specify Person/Department, e.g., Your Manager, HR Department, AI Policy Lead].

GenAI Brainstorming Exercise: The Creative Matrix

Brainstorming exercise adapted from Google Cloud Skills Boost .

It can sometimes be tough to brainstorm new gen AI use cases and determine which ones to tackle. One method we recommend trying is the creative matrix.

A creative matrix is great because it can help your team think through different aspects of what gen AI can do. For this creative matrix workflow, you would get a team together and create two axes. The axes are a bit flexible, but we can give you some ideas:

  • On one axis, you might write the different options for gen AI solutions. This can be organized by gen AI product, modality (e.g., text, image, code, audio), or another dimension.
  • On the other axis, you would write out things that are specific to your business. This can be business personas (e.g., marketing manager, customer service agent, operations lead), workflows (e.g., content creation, customer support, product design), or strategic goals (e.g., increase efficiency, drive innovation, enhance customer experience).

Then, have the team write sticky notes (either virtually or in person) with different ideas of how the two axes can intersect and place them in the right spot. This should get you to think creatively about how your organization can leverage gen AI to truly help your business priorities.
